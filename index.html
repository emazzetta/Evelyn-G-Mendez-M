Red lentil, chickpea & chilli soup



Red lentil, chickpea and chilli soup offers a delicious and nutritious option for anyone seeking a healthy meal.

Red lentils are rich in fibre, which supports digestion and helps maintain stable energy levels. Chickpeas provide plant-based protein, making the soup filling and ideal for vegetarians.

The chilli adds warmth and can boost metabolism, as well as bringing a wonderfully bold flavour. This soup is also naturally low in fat and easy to prepare.
It’s perfect for cold days, affordable, and can be made in large batches.

Overall, it’s a comforting, satisfying choice that promotes wellbeing and balanced nutrition. The original recipe can be found here.



Ingredients

  • 2 tsp cumin seeds
  • large pinch chilli flakes
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 red onion
  • 850ml vegetable stock
  • 400g can tomatoes
  • 200g can chickpeas
  • small bunch coriander
  • 4 tbsp 0% Greek yogurt


Method

  1. Heat a large saucepan and dry-fry 2 tsp cumin seeds and a large pinch of chilli flakes for 1 min, or until they start to jump around the pan and release their aromas.
  2. Add 1 tbsp olive oil and 1 chopped red onion, and cook for 5 mins.
  3. Stir in 140g red split lentils, 850ml vegetable stock or water and a 400g can tomatoes, then bring to the boil. Simmer for 15 mins until the lentils have softened.
  4. Whizz the soup with a stick blender or in a food processor until it is a rough purée, pour back into the pan and add a 200g can drained and rinsed chickpeas.
  5. Heat gently, season well and stir in a small bunch of chopped coriander, reserving a few leaves to serve. Finish with 4 tbsp 0% Greek yogurt and extra coriander leaves.
